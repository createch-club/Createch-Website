CAN MARS BE MADE HABITABLE?

User May 13, 2022

Since the late 1940s, as part of the larger study of Mars, the notion of transporting humans to Mars has been the topic of Aerospace Engineering and Scientific Investigations. The Martian moons Phobos and Deimos have also been suggested for exploration.

Many missions were launched for this purpose, some of which failed miserably and others which succeeded.

MARINER 9: On May 30, 1971, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) launched Mariner 9 towards Mars. On November 14, 1971, it arrived at Mars and became the first spacecraft to circle another planet. On October 27, 1970, Mariner 9 was decommissioned, but it was projected to stay in orbit until 2022.

VIKING 1 and 2: The VIKING 1 and 2 spacecraft were each made up of an orbiter and a lander. VIKING 1 was launched on August 20, 1975, and the VIKING lander was deployed to the surface of Mars on July 200, 1976, following a month of looking for landing locations. The first successful Mars landing. On September 9, 1975, the VIKING 2 spacecraft was launched, and its lander landed on September 3, 1976.

JAPAN'S NOZOMI MARS PROBE: On July 4, 1998, Japan launched the Nozomi spacecraft, the country's first planetary mission. The spacecraft, however, was unable to reach Mars as anticipated due to a failing valve on December 20, 1998.

SIGNS OF HABITABILITY ON MARS

In 3 billion-year-old rocks deposited on the bottom of Gale Crater, NASA's nuclear-powered MARS CURIOSITY ROVER discovered a range of organic molecules, a need for life as we know it on Earth.

Scientists discovered a seasonal change in methane levels in the Martian atmosphere in a second, perhaps more significant discovery. From winter to summer, background levels increase by a factor of three, indicating that the gas is released repeatedly. Because life is responsible for 95% of the methane found on Earth, a seasonal variation in methane levels is fascinating. Furthermore, methane is a short-lived gas. So, if we see methane in the Martian atmosphere, that means something is happening today, it’s being releases or it’s being created.


MY VIEWPOINT

Everyone seems to be thinking about Mars these days. NASA hopes to transport humans to the planet by 2030, but SpaceX wants to get there even sooner, by 2024.

Mars' atmosphere is mainly carbon dioxide, the planet's surface is too frigid for human existence, and the planet's gravity is just 38% that of Earth. Furthermore, the atmosphere on Mars is only 1% of the Earth's atmosphere at sea level. It's difficult to get to the surface because of this. How is NASA going to get there? How can we hope to survive in the face of such odds? As a result, it could or might not be able to thrive there.

FUTURE PREDICTIONS

No one can say for sure what Mars will look like as the remainder of the century progresses. We just know that humanity will make it to Mars, and our aim is to stay and construct a new home for ourselves.
